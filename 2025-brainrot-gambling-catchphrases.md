## Key Trends & Slang

### **Italian Brainrot**
The dominant brainrot phenomenon of 2025 featuring AI-generated characters:
- **Tralalero Tralala** - A shark with Nike sneakers
- **Ballerina Cappuccina** - A ballerina with a cappuccino cup head
- **Bombardiro Crocodilo** - A crocodile-headed military airplane
- **Tung Tung Tung Sahur** - Indonesian-origin character that became a meme icon

These characters dominated TikTok with 55+ million views and spawned trading card games, Roblox games, and merchandise.

### **67 (Six Seven)**
**Dictionary.com's 2025 Word of the Year**
- Pronounced "six seven" NOT "sixty-seven"
- Has no fixed meaning - it's pure energy and vibes
- Accompanied by a hand gesture (palms up, moving in see-saw motion)
- Can mean "so-so," "maybe," or absolutely nothing
- Origins: Skrilla's song "Doot Doot (6 7)" and basketball player LaMelo Ball (who is 6'7")

### **Skibidi**
- From the viral "Skibidi Toilet" YouTube series
- Means basically anything or nothing depending on context
- Can be good, bad, cool, or just random nonsense
- Often combined with other slang: "Skibidi Rizz," "Skibidi Ohio"

### **Ohio**
- Slang for something bizarre, cursed, or chaotic
- "Only in Ohio" = something so weird it feels unreal
- Not meant literally - represents peak weirdness in meme culture
- "Ohio Rizz" = awkward/unusual charm

### **Rizz**
- Charisma, charm, or flirting ability
- "He's got rizz" = he's smooth with the ladies
- "Rizzler" = someone with exceptional rizz
- Origins: Popularized by streamer Kai Cenat

### **Gyatt/Gyat**
- Expression of strong excitement, surprise, or admiration
- Also means "big butt" (acronym: "Girl, your ass is thick")
- Pronunciation: [gee-yot]
- Often used as "Gyatt damn!"

### **Sigma**
- Originally: independent, successful, alpha-type male
- Now also means "excellent" or "cool"
- "What the sigma?" = "What the heck?"
- Part of "sigma male grindset" culture (often used ironically)

### **Other Essential Slang**
- **Bussin'** - Exceptionally good, usually about food
- **Cap/No Cap** - Lie/No lie, truth
- **Slay** - Killing it, being impressive
- **Based** - Being true to yourself, authentic
- **Mid** - Mediocre, average, not impressive
- **NPC** - Someone who acts robotically or unaware
- **Crashout** - To throw a fit or have a meltdown
- **Fanum Tax** - Stealing food from a friend
- **Mewing** - Working on strengthening your jawline
- **Aura** - Your vibe, presence, or charisma level
- **Chopped** - Bad, ruined, messed up

## Age-Appropriate Usage Notes

### **Target Audience**
- **Gen Z** (born 1997-2012): Ages 13-28 in 2025
- **Gen Alpha** (born 2010-2025): Ages 0-15 in 2025

**IMPORTANT:** For gambling sites, your legal audience is 18+ or 21+ depending on jurisdiction. This means targeting the older end of Gen Z (18-28 year olds) who still relate to these memes.

### **Tone Guidelines**
1. **Don't overdo it** - Use brainrot sparingly or it becomes cringe
2. **Mix with normalcy** - Balance meme language with clear, professional copy
3. **Stay current** - These trends change fast; update every 2-3 months
4. **Be authentic** - Forced slang is instantly spotted and mocked
5. **Test with audience** - What's funny to Gen Alpha (kids) might be cringe to Gen Z (adults)

## Humor Style Characteristics

### **What Makes Gen Z/Alpha Humor Different:**
1. **Absurdism** - Randomness IS the joke
2. **Post-Irony** - Multiple layers of irony; joke within a joke
3. **Anti-Humor** - Absence of a punchline becomes the punchline
4. **Speed** - Memes mutate in days, not weeks
5. **Self-Referential** - Memes about memes
6. **Chaos** - Unpredictable, nonsensical combinations
7. **Vulnerability** - Combines humor with real emotions
8. **Coping Mechanism** - Laughing at chaos to stay sane

### **Common Formats:**
- Text-heavy memes on colored backgrounds
- Surreal AI-generated imagery
- Hyper-specific relatable moments
- Rapid tone shifts (wholesome â†’ chaotic)
- "POV" style content

## Warning: Quick Death Scenarios

### **What Kills Memes:**
1. **Parents/Teachers using them** - Instant cringe
2. **Corporate forced usage** - Feels inauthentic
3. **Overuse** - Beating a dead horse
4. **Misuse** - Getting the context wrong
5. **Mainstreaming** - Once everyone knows, it's dead

### **Safe Usage:**
- Use as accent, not foundation
- Mix old and new slang
- Stay slightly behind the curve (what's trending NOW will be dead when you launch)
- Have a young team member vet your copy
- Be ready to pivot quickly
